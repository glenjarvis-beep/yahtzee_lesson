💬 Discussion Prompts

Ask the class: "We just rolled — what's the probability of getting this exact outcome again?"
Think about it: "If we recorded 100 turns, how many Yahtzees would probability theory predict?"
Deeper thinking: "Why do our real results sometimes differ from what the maths says should happen?"

📐 Key Probability Facts

🎲 P(one specific number on 1 die) = 1/6 ≈ 16.7%
🎲 P(any even number on 1 die) = 3/6 = 50%
🎲 P(Yahtzee on first roll only) = 1/1296 ≈ 0.08%
🎲 P(Yahtzee across a 3-roll turn) = ≈ 4.6%
More trials → results closer to theory (Law of Large Numbers)
